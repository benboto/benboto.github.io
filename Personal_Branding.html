Personal Branding

Project Type: Personal

Status: Open Ended

Time Period: ~ 2 days, Summer 2015

Tools Used: Adobe Illustrator

This project was mostly recreational for me, based on the nickname "Benbot", which I often use as an online pseudonym. It began as a simple exercise in logo design, something I enjoy very much and often do for fun.

Simple graphics appeal to me (as you may be able to see, looking at my website) and I have become very fond of 2 color logos, with one color and a shade of black or white to contrast. I find it a very clean and clear method of presenting a design idea.

As the designs progressed however, I became very attached to them and decided to polish the idea further. In particular, the robot head character appealed to me (inspired by a Lego creation of mine). I tried out multiple quirky iterations before simplifying the design.

Next, I expanded the logo to include the name pf the brand, including a stylized underline to hold the elements together.

As I iterated on the theme, I sought feedback from some close friends (from the same University Program). Feedback was quite helpful, especially on this project, when iterations are frequent and small and you can begin to lose track of what the design looks like as a whole.

The feedback helped me focus on some important high level changes, so I grudgingly abandoned the top hat for a more traditonal robot look. Many antenna iterations followed, but the final design (below) is simple and clever. At least, I think so anyway.

The final branding includes a logo with and without text, for flexible usage. The antenna creates a stylized 'B', a small detail, but it ties the logo to the text and written brand name.

I am very satisfied with the outcome of this endeavour and I intend to use this branding to promote future personal and professional independent projects under the Benbot pseudonym.

This project was so enjoyable that I did not want to just stop, so I adapted the logo for a variety of themes and holidays! While this was mostly for fun, this gives me the ability to tailor my branding more specifically than I would with a static logo.

Plus, they look pretty darn cute.

WIP - Ben Carson 2015
