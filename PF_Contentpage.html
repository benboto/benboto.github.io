THE PROJECT

Pulse Fighters is the result of almost a year of hard work by a group of IMD fourth year students. An arcade brawler style game, akin to Super Smash Brothers or Playstation All-Stars, but with unique player-game interactions designed to enhance the game's accessibility and engage players on more levels.

The Pulse Fighters project combines three core components into a single game experience. First, the game itself, built in Unity. Second, the Pulse Sensor and Tinyduino processor, which reads the player’s heart beat. Third, the Android-based mobile phone controller, allowing players to interact with the game using a simple 3 button and one control stick layout.

The core gameplay involves two to four players competing against each other, each choosing from one of four playable characters and battling it out on a variety of game levels. Once in game, players jump, punch and battle within a 2D plane in a 3D game environment. All gameplay is viewed from a side-view perspective, with a camera that dynamically tracks the players on the screen as they move about the stage. Each character has unique attacks, animations and audio cues. During the course of the game, the players’ heart rate will be monitored and should the game determine that one or more of the players are not fully engaged, it will trigger a variety of dynamic game events.
