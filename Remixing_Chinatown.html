REMIXING CHINATOWN

Project Type: Adademic - Team Based

Status: Complete

Time Period: ~ 1 Month, Summer Term 2014

Tools Used: Sublime Text 2, Adobe Illustrator

Remixing Chinatown was a joint project between 3 IMD (my program) students and 2 Journalism students. The project was the culmination of our shared 'Advanced Topics' course, which highlighted the merging of journalism with modern media technology.

The website itself is an interactive article, taking visitors on a trip through Ottawa's Chinatown and exploring some of the interesting people and places that make it unique. While myself and the other IMD students worked on desiging and building the website, the Journalism students collected interviews and images and research. Together we created a vision for the project and brought it all together.

The project was not perfect, but it was an opportunity to explore web design more and experiment with interesting content and layout. You can check out the full website here.

Much of my work on the project was the design, creation and iteration of the navigation and interactive elements on the page. I also contributed to the overall design of the site, from the visual aesthetics to the usability.

I also created a series of wireframes during the concept and design phase of the project. These helped influence the final product and gave direction to the team members when starting to build the site. Not all of the concepts made it to the submitted version, many were changed and some were dropped because of content, time or knowledge limitations - but everything has to start somewhere.

The project was a very useful lesson in working with people, not only with different strengths, but also from different disciplines. Learning to allocate tasks appropriately and collaborate with people who have a different knowledge background was a new and valuable experience.
